Attack Path Modeling in Segmented Enterprise Networks

This analysis maps how attackers traverse segmented network environments using legitimate credentials, trusted tools, and protocol abuse. Rather than exploiting perimeter weaknesses, modern lateral movement relies on BloodHound-visible privilege relationships that defenders rarely audit proactively.

  • Tier-0 asset exposure through transitive group membership in 6 of 8 lab simulations
  • AS-REP Roasting effective against default configurations in all tested environments
  • Constrained delegation abuse consistently underdetected across Elastic and Splunk baselines
  • GenericWrite permissions mapped to 3+ privilege escalation paths in each AD structure analyzed
Read Full Analysis →
KEY FINDING

In segmented environments, the most reliable lateral movement paths are not firewall bypasses — they are misconfigured delegation settings and over-provisioned service accounts that exist in nearly every production AD deployment.

METHODOLOGY

Lab recreations of 4 enterprise AD topologies. Each environment built from real architectural patterns, then analyzed using BloodHound CE, manual enumeration, and custom detection evaluation scripts.
