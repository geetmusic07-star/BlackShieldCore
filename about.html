Mission & Focus

About
BlackShield Core.

A personal cybersecurity and AI experimentation platform focused on depth over breadth. Every lab, tool, and writeup exists because the author worked through the scenario and wanted to leave a legible trail behind.

BlackShield Core is a solo project and a public notebook. It collects hands-on labs, small browser tools, and long-form technical writing into one place — organized the way the author wishes everyone else's security blogs were organized.

The bar for publishing anything here is a simple question: is this useful to someone who already knows the basics and is trying to go deeper? If the answer is no, it doesn't ship. That keeps the catalog small. It also keeps it honest.

The site deliberately does not try to be a training vendor, a certification track, or a SOC-as-a-service. It is a working knowledge base. Features are gated by whether they are actually finished. Everything marked Build Stage or Planned is visible precisely so you can see what is and isn't real yet.

Operating Principles
01
Depth over breadth.
One thorough writeup is worth ten surface-level posts. If the material doesn't push past Stack Overflow answers and marketing blogs, it isn't worth writing. Every piece should answer a question you couldn't easily answer by searching.
02
No overclaim.
The site does not have live connections, real-time threat intelligence, a 24/7 SOC, or any of the theater that usually decorates security sites. Labs are self-contained. Status badges reflect reality: Available, Build Stage, Planned.
03
Legible by design.
Every post has a visible structure — TOC, callouts, clearly marked code blocks, related content. A reader should be able to jump in at any point and orient themselves in under thirty seconds.
04
Client-side where possible.
Tools run in your browser. There's no account system, no telemetry on tool data, and no server that sees your tokens or passwords. Content is static HTML — readable, linkable, archivable.
Focus Areas
Web & API Security
Authentication flows, session handling, JWTs, OAuth, IDOR, SSRF, cache deception, modern framework pitfalls.
AI / LLM Security
Prompt injection taxonomy, indirect injection, classifier evasion, RAG poisoning, and what AI security can't do.
Active Directory
Kerberos attacks, ACL paths, delegation abuse, tiered admin models, and realistic AD hardening tradeoffs.
Detection Engineering
Sigma rule quality, telemetry gap analysis, log correlation patterns, and detection-as-code in realistic SOCs.
Incident Response
Anonymized post-incident breakdowns, timeline reconstruction, and what actually goes wrong in the first 48 hours.
Threat Modeling
Practical, stakeholder-legible threat modeling — without the LARP of ten-page STRIDE diagrams nobody reads.
Scope
What this site is
  • A personal, curated catalog of security labs and writeups
  • An outlet for research that doesn't fit into a vendor blog or academic paper
  • A place to link from resumes, talks, and interviews with real evidence of work
  • A reference for people who want depth on specific topics
What this site isn't
  • A bootcamp, certification track, or replacement for formal training
  • A live threat intel feed or SOC service
  • A pentesting company — engagements referenced in case studies are anonymized
  • A news site — content is slower and more considered by design
